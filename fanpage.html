The greatest set on the greatest stage...Wembley Stadium in 1985

Their iconic status, in question only by the skeptical few, was cemented by their performance at Live Aid in 1985. An all day concert, Live Aid was a music industry telethon designed to raise awareness of, and money for, victims of the famine in Africa. With stages in the US (JFK Stadium in Philadelphia) and the UK (Wembley Stadium in London), highlighted by super acts like David Bowie, U2, and Elton John, Queen was relegated to the 7pm slot, well before sunset on a very warm day in July!! Even the sun couldn't outshine the most memorable performance of the '80s, on the largest stage in music history.
